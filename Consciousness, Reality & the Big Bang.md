# Consciousness, Reality & the Big Bang
### A Friday Morning Rabbit Hole — May 15, 2026

## Starting Point: The Claim

> *"The Big Bang is nothing more than the emergence of a common consciousness."*

This conversation began with a ZeroHedge article referencing several real scientific papers and researchers, and a YouTube transcript featuring Thomas Campbell and Robert Lanza. The goal: unpack what's legitimate science, what's legitimate speculation, and what's narrative inflation.

## The Core Scientific Source

### Maria Strømme — Uppsala University (2025)
**Paper:** *"Universal consciousness as foundational field: A theoretical bridge between quantum physics and non-dual philosophy"*
**Journal:** *AIP Advances* (peer-reviewed, American Institute of Physics)
**DOI:** 10.1063/5.0290984

Key claims:
- Consciousness is not a byproduct of brain activity — it is a **fundamental field** underlying matter, space, and time
- The Big Bang was not the origin of everything, but a **transformation within a pre-existing field of consciousness**
- Individual minds are localized "excitations" of that universal field — like ripples in a deeper ocean
- Draws on quantum field theory, symmetry breaking, and non-dual philosophy (particularly Advaita Vedanta)

**Notable:** Strømme is a nanotechnology expert, not a philosopher. The paper was selected as best paper of its issue and featured on the journal cover. It passed peer review — though that means it merits serious engagement, not automatic acceptance.

## The Supporting Frameworks

### 1. Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR Theory
- Proposes consciousness originates at the **quantum level inside neurons**, specifically in microtubule structures
- Combines quantum gravity, molecular biology, and neuroscience
- Status: **not falsified, not confirmed** — lives in the uncomfortable purgatory of "unfalsifiable-ish"
- A 2024 University of Maryland study showed stabilizing microtubules in rats delayed loss of consciousness under anesthesia — consistent with but not proof of Orch-OR
- Main criticism: decoherence timescales in warm biological systems make sustained quantum coherence implausible

### 2. Robert Lanza — Biocentrism
- Serious credentials: stem cell pioneer, named among Time's 100 most influential people
- Core claim: **life and consciousness are not products of the universe — the universe is a product of consciousness**
- Draws on the quantum observer effect: particles have no definite properties until observed
- Key question he raises: why do we assume the universe existed before any observer was present to collapse probabilities?
- His leap — from "particles need observation to have definite states" to "consciousness precedes physical reality" — is philosophically coherent but not empirically proven

### 3. Thomas Campbell — My Big TOE (Theory of Everything)
- PhD physicist, former NASA researcher
- Developed his framework largely through Monroe Institute out-of-body exploration work
- Describes reality as a **Larger Consciousness System (LCS)**: a multiplayer simulation that renders experience on demand
- Individual humans are "units of consciousness" temporarily connected to physical avatars
- Death = logging out; consciousness returns to the system, re-engages in new scenarios
- **Important caveat:** My Big TOE is self-published and not peer-reviewed. Internally consistent as a model, but consistency ≠ truth

### 4. Bohm & Pribram — The Holographic Universe
- David Bohm (quantum physicist, colleague of Einstein): reality as we perceive it is a projection of a deeper **implicate order** — a non-local field beyond space and time where everything is connected
- Karl Pribram (neuroscientist): memory in the brain behaves **holographically** — distributed, non-local, interference-based rather than stored in fixed locations
- Together: the external world may be a projection emerging from a deeper field of consciousness
- A hologram's key property: every fragment contains the whole image — suggesting fundamental interconnectedness

## The NDE Evidence

This is arguably the most empirically grounded and hardest-to-dismiss thread.

### Sam Parnia — AWARE Study
- Studied hundreds of cases of clinical death (no heartbeat, no brain activity)
- Published findings: 39% of survivors reported awareness or memories during clinical death; 20% had transcendent experiences; 3.5% had verified visual awareness
- One documented case: a patient accurately described medical procedures performed on his body while his brain showed flatlined activity
- Parnia's own position is careful: consciousness appears to continue in the **early minutes/hours** after clinical death — he does not claim to know what happens beyond that window

### Bruce Greyson — University of Virginia
- One of the most respected figures in NDE research
- Common verified themes: floating outside the body, observing medical procedures, traveling through light
- Many cases involve patients reporting details — conversations in other rooms, visual specifics — they could not physically have known

### Terminal Lucidity
- Documented phenomenon: Alzheimer's patients with severely deteriorated brains becoming **fully coherent** moments before death — recognizing loved ones, holding conversations — then passing
- ~80 cases documented in literature over a century; increasing research in the last decade
- Genuinely unexplained by the "brain generates consciousness" model
- If the brain is the hardware and it's destroyed, how is the software suddenly running clearly?

## The Critical Dissent

### Vlatko Vedral — Oxford Physicist (Many-Worlds Interpretation)
The most important counter-voice, included in the original ZeroHedge article itself:

> *"The universe does not wait for humans to notice something before making a decision. The interaction itself is what matters."*

His key point: the quantum "observer" does **not** mean a conscious mind. Any physical interaction — including a rock hitting a particle — constitutes "observation" in the quantum sense. The wave function collapses through physical interaction, not through awareness.

This is a crucial distinction the Campbell/Lanza framework tends to blur.

## Two Personal Intuitions That Map to Real Philosophy

### 1. "An apple is an apple because we decided to call it that"
This is **linguistic relativity** and **social constructivism** — most associated with Wittgenstein:
- Language doesn't just describe reality; it structures how we perceive and carve it up
- Different languages create different experiential realities (the classic example: Indigenous languages with dozens of words for snow)
- The word is socially constructed. The falling of the apple is not.
- The question: where exactly is the line between the map (language, shared agreement) and the territory (what's actually out there)?
- Physics keeps finding that the "territory" gets stranger the deeper you look

### 2. "I know of the Pacific Ocean but I've never seen it — is it real?"
This maps to **quantum Bayesianism (QBism)** and the **holographic principle**:
- The Pacific Ocean almost certainly exists independently — other observers confirm it, it has causal effects
- But *your* Pacific Ocean is a consensus hallucination assembled from secondhand information
- Scale that up: every person operates on a model of reality that is almost entirely secondhand
- You've directly perceived a vanishingly small fraction of what you "know" exists
- The rest is trust in other observers, instruments, and shared language

**The uncomfortable synthesis:** If reality is partly constructed through language and agreement, and the universe only "renders" what's being observed, and most of your reality is a model built from consensus rather than direct experience — then simulation theory stops being a quirky thought experiment and starts looking like a description of what's already happening.

## The Deeper Question

All of these frameworks are circling the same ancient problem, now dressed in modern physics:

**The Mind-Body Problem**

| Position | Claim |
|---|---|
| Materialism | Mind emerges from matter. Consciousness is what a sufficiently complex brain does. |
| Idealism | Matter emerges from mind. The physical world is what consciousness produces or experiences. |

Neither has been proven. Both have serious defenders. The fact that we've argued about this since at least Descartes — and that cutting-edge physics keeps hitting the same wall — suggests this may be one of those questions where the asking is more valuable than any answer currently available.

**Max Planck, founder of quantum theory:**
> *"I regard consciousness as fundamental. Everything else derives from it — not the other way around."*

## The Philosophical Lineage

Strømme's paper explicitly bridges to **non-dual philosophy** — specifically traditions like Advaita Vedanta, which has argued for thousands of years that:
- Individual consciousness (Atman) is identical to the universal ground of being (Brahman)
- The material world is a secondary manifestation of that ground

This isn't a 2025 discovery. It's ancient Indian metaphysics dressed in quantum field equations. Western parallels include Schopenhauer's Will, Hegel's Geist, and Whitehead's process philosophy. Schrödinger himself — one of the founders of quantum mechanics — was deeply influenced by Vedanta.

## Where the Video Loses the Thread

The YouTube transcript (featuring Campbell and Lanza) contains real science but overstates its conclusions:

1. **"Observer" ≠ conscious mind** — the video repeatedly conflates any physical interaction (quantum observer) with conscious awareness. Vedral's dissent directly addresses this.
2. **Campbell's framework is not peer-reviewed** — presented with the same weight as Parnia's published research, which is misleading.
3. **Parnia's careful hedging gets stripped away** — his published position is that consciousness appears to persist in early minutes post-death. The video runs that all the way to reincarnation and parallel reality shifts.
4. **It ends with a numerology ad** — a meaningful signal about the video's primary purpose.
