## Part 2 — Geomagnetic Pole Reversal: The Evidence

### Three independent lines of proof

#### 1. Seafloor Magnetic Striping
Mid-ocean ridges constantly extrude new lava which cools and permanently locks in the magnetic field direction of that moment. The seafloor is a continuous magnetic tape recording stretching back hundreds of millions of years. Crucially, the stripes are **perfectly symmetric** on both sides of ridges — impossible to fake, ruling out any local explanation.

#### 2. Lava Flow Records (paleomagnetism)
Iron-rich minerals in cooling lava act as compasses, freezing field direction permanently. Discovered in the 1920s by Japanese geologist Motonori Matuyama, who noticed older lava flows pointed *south* instead of north. Confirmed globally by the 1960s — reversals happen simultaneously worldwide.

#### 3. Deep-Sea Sediment Cores
Microscopic magnetic particles settle continuously through the water column, aligning with the field as they land. A sediment core is an unbroken magnetic diary with no gaps, going back millions of years.

**All three methods produce the same reversal timeline independently. That convergence is the proof.**

### Key events in the reversal record
| Event | Approximate Date | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Brunhes-Matuyama boundary | 780,000 years ago | Last full reversal — north became south and stayed |
| Laschamp Excursion | 41,000 years ago | Brief reversal lasting ~440 years; field dropped to ~5% of normal |
| Current epoch | Now | Field declining ~5% per century; at lowest strength in 41,000 years |

### Current precursors
- **Magnetic north pole acceleration**: moved ~10 km/yr 1900–1990; now moving 60+ km/yr toward Siberia

- **Lake Agassiz collapse**: a glacial lake larger than all the modern Great Lakes combined discharged into the North Atlantic, shutting down thermohaline circulation (the ocean's conveyor belt) and plunging the Northern Hemisphere back into cold for 1,200 years

## Part 7 — El Niño, La Niña, and Solar Ocean Heating

### The official model
- La Niña: trade winds push warm water west, heat accumulates in western Pacific
- El Niño: winds weaken, heat releases eastward, global temperatures spike
- **NASA's admission**: what triggers the wind shift remains unknown after decades of study

### The 2023–24 anomaly
- Global surface temps hit 1.58°C above pre-industrial levels
- A 0.36°C year-over-year rise — normally takes 1–2 decades
- Warming in the tropical North Atlantic was so anomalous it broke every predictive model

### The solar/magnetic connection

**Direct solar heating**: Solar Cycle 25 was predicted to be weak and is instead tracking as one of the strongest in a century. Peak: 2024–2025. More energy hitting ocean surfaces directly during the exact El Niño window.

**The Svensmark/Umbrella Effect**: A weakening magnetic field allows more galactic cosmic rays into the atmosphere, which seed low cloud formation, which increases albedo (reflectivity) and should cause cooling. Published in *Nature* (2017). The fact that we're warming despite this suggests a heat source powerful enough to overwhelm the cosmic ray cooling — almost certainly the combination of a strong solar cycle hitting a weak magnetic shield.

**The ratchet effect**: Each La Niña cycle stores more heat than the last (baseline rising). Each El Niño discharges hotter. One-directional. The ocean as a heat battery that charges faster than it discharges.
